# Analyze Tab Logic Breakdown
The **Analyze** tab acts as the primary deep-dive dashboard for a single asset (e.g., AAPL). It queries the `GET /analyze/{symbol}` backend endpoint, which runs a full inference pipeline through the multi-agent system. Below is the logic behind every visible value and component.

### 1. Regime Panel (`RegimePanel.jsx`)
- **What it represents:** The current "State" of the market (Trending, Mean-Reverting, High-Volatility).
T:-Price keeps moving in one direction (up or down)
M:-Price keeps coming back to an average value
H:-Price moves wildly and unpredictably
- **Backend Logic:** Calculated by the `market_regime_agent.py`. The backend runs a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) to classify the historical volatility and returns. It also runs Bayesian Online Changepoint Detection (BOCPD) to calculate the `changepoint_probability`, which detects sudden structural shifts in the market.
- **Displayed Values:** 
  - *Dominant Regime:* The state with the highest probability.
  - *Confidence Bars:* The exact softmax probabilities assigned to each regime by the HMM.

### 2. Sentiment Panel (`SentimentPanel.jsx`)
- **What it represents:** Qualitative text-based data translated into quantitative numerical scores.
- **Backend Logic:** Compiled by `data_fetcher.py`.
  - *News Sentiment:* Fetches recent articles from NewsAPI, running them through a FinBERT NLP model. Yields a score from -1.0 (Bearish) to 1.0 (Bullish).
  - *Reddit Sentiment:* Parses subreddit trends (e.g., WallStreetBets) looking for keyword mentions and producing a Z-Score mapping social hype.
  - *SEC Flags:* Checks for upcoming earnings dates or sudden 8-K regulatory filings outlining material corporate changes.
  Detecting important official company events.
  8-K:- An urgent report companies must file when something major happens.

### 3. Macro Context (`MacroPanel.jsx`)
- **What it represents:** The broader economic baseline.
- **Backend Logic:** Queries the FRED API database.
  - *VIX / Market Fear:* Tracks the S&P 500 volatility index.
  - *Yield Curve Spread *(T10Y2Y)*:* The difference between 10-year and 2-year Treasury bonds. An inverted curve dictates imminent macroscopic recessions.
  Normal (Positive Spread) :- Long-term yield > short-term
  Economy is healthy
  Inverted (Negative Spread):- Short-term > long-term
  Strong signal of recession coming

  *Credit Spread (HYG/LQD):* The ratio of High-Yield (Junk) bonds to Investment Grade bonds. Evaluates institutional risk appetite.
  High Spread :- Risky
  Low Spread :- Safe
  HYG :-Junk bonds (risky companies, higher returns)
  LQD:- Safe bonds (strong companies, lower returns)
  If you feel confident:
→ You buy risky bonds (HYG)
If you feel scared:
→ You buy safe bonds (LQD)

### 4. Machine Learning Vectors (`AgentSignals.jsx` & `StateVector.jsx`)
- **What it represents:** The intermediate votes from the micro-agents before reaching the final RL model.
- **Displayed Values:**
  - *Technical Indicators:* Moving Average crossovers and RSI logic predicting raw momentum.
  - *Vector Snapshot:* A readout of the raw 45-dimensional numerical float tensor generated by `feature_pipeline.py` which is ultimately fed into the neural network.

### 5. Trade Decision (`TradeDecision.jsx`)
- **What it represents:** The final verdict output.
- **Backend Logic:** Driven by `self_critique_agent.py` and the RL Meta-Controller.
  - *Kelly Fraction:* Uses the Kelly Criterion formula `(WinRate - (1-WinRate)/RewardRiskRatio)` to suggest optimal mathematical bet sizing that maximizes compound growth without facing total ruin.
  - *Veto Reason:* A safety check. Even if the RL agent wants to buy, the Critique agent checks CVaR (Conditional Value at Risk:How bad can losses get in worst-case scenarios?) and SEC constraints. If a risk threshold is broken, the trade is rejected entirely or the position size is slashed.
  - *Stop Loss / Take Profit:* Volatility-adjusted bounds mathematically plotted to lock in gains or cut losses dynamically based on current ATR (Average True Range).

### 6. Disagreement Heatmap (`DisagreementHeatmap.jsx`)
- **What it represents:** How "confused" or "divided" the AI models are.
- **Displayed Values:**
  - *Epistemic Uncertainty:* Uncertainty from a lack of historical data (e.g. trading during a black swan event).
  - *Aleatoric Uncertainty:* Inherent market noise (it's impossible to predict perfectly).
  - *Consensus:* A calculated standard deviation across a DeepEnsemble predicting the price. If models vote in vastly different directions, consensus drops, increasing the disagreement penalty applied later.
